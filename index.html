Mission

Many science competitions reward access to funding, lab space, or outside mentorship rather than ideas. Project EqualSpark removes those barriers.

Students are evaluated on their reasoning, hypotheses, and creativity.

Essentially, they will submit an idea that they have within their field of choosing, and expound their plan to build something to fix a real problem. They are not judged on not equipment or prototypes, creating a fair and accessible space for all students.
