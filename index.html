Redefining What It Means to Do Science

Traditional science competitions can unintentionally reward access to money, labs, and mentors. Project EqualSpark flips the model—judging ideas, reasoning, and creativity rather than physical builds.

Equity

No participant should need money, lab access, or specialized equipment to compete.

Intellectual Merit

How It Works

EqualSpark is idea-based. You explain your thinking and plan—execution and expensive builds are not required.

1

Pick a category + idea type

Choose one of the six categories, then define your submission (question/hypothesis, solution concept, experimental design, etc.).

Solo or team (up to 3) Grades 7–8
2

Develop your reasoning

Explain assumptions, variables, constraints, and feasibility. If relevant, describe how you would test or build it—without actually executing.

3

Submit + get evaluated

Submit in one of three accessible formats. Judges score scientific thinking, creativity, feasibility, impact, and clarity.

Judging Criteria

Scientific thinking 30%
Creativity & originality 25%
Feasibility 20%
Social / real-world impact 15%
Communication clarity 10%

Note: Submissions are judged on thinking—not production value. Microgrants (up to $1,000) must be used for learning/creation in the student’s field. Parents/guardians must provide receipts for purchases.
